FAQ

Got questions about HackKean? Read on.

  • What's a hackathon?

    It’s a grand gathering of people to make their ideas come to life. Think out of the box, try new technologies, ask questions, make mistakes, discover a new language, and have fun!

  • Who, When, & Where?

    HackKean III will run for 24 hours on April 1st & 2nd at Kean University's North Ave Academic Building, open to all students! (if you are under 18, we'll need parent/guardian consent form)

  • How do I sign up?

    Interested? Registration will be open soon! Subscribe here to participate in HackKean III.

  • How's parking & travel reimbursement?

    Unfortunately, we currently cannot offer travel reimbursements to hackers. Parking is available in front of North Ave Academic Building (white lines)

  • What kind of project can I work on?

    Whatever you'd like. Software, hardware, design, music, art, animation — take your pick. In addition to traditional prizing for top projects, we'll be awarding prizes to best projects in design, IoT, and more.

  • Teams?

    Teams should be groups of up to 4 students. You can also work alone, but we encourage collaborating with the other great minds at HackKean.

  • Do I need to have a finished project by the end?

    Your goal should be to have a finished or almost complete project by Sunday. However, HackKean is ultimately a learning experience, so don't sweat it too much if you can't finish.

  • I haven't been to a hackthon before...?

    That’s absolutely fine! We encourage beginners to join us. Hit us up if you've got any questions not answered here.

  • What should I bring?

    Whatever you need to create. Hardware, computers, machines, and whatever else. Don't forget hygeine. We'll be providing some hardware first-come, first-served.

  • What shouldn't I bring?

    A bad attitude. Come with an open mind & get ready to build.

  • I'm not a college student. Can I participate?

    We're open to high school students! (parent/guardian must sign waiver) In the industry? If you're interested in being a mentor, drop us a line.

  • How can I get in touch?

    Have a question not answered here? Shoot us an email, a message on Facebook, or a a DM.

  • What is your code of conduct?

    View our Code of Conduct, set forth by Major League Hacking, here.
